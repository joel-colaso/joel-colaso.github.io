Ingenuity Inspired Drone



Ingenuity is a small helicopter landed by NASA on Mars in 2020 that has two coaxial helices and took images of Mars during 72 missions blowing the expected 5 out of the water.


During my second year at E.N.S.E.A. I worked alongside 3 of my peers, Kevin Dugard, Elio Flandin, and Pénélope Henner to design and build a drone inspired but made to fly on on Earth's atmosphere


Briefly, our drone used a Raspberry Pi Zero card to receive instructions by WiFi (SSH) from the GUI in a computer. Then it passed the instructions to a STM32 microcontroller which then decoded it and then controlled two motors and used a distance sensor to control the height of the helicopter.



My main responsability was writing the code for controlling the helicopter with the STM32 microcontroller


I designed the instruction format for communication between the Raspberry PI and the STM32 microcontrollers and developped the UART reception and decoding of the instructions.


I also developped the firmware to control the motors, which employed Pulse Width Modulation (PWM) to determine the power of the motors.



Here is an image of the final appearance of our chassis. The bases employ laser cutting and the other parts were 3D printed and painted.
